# From Emotional Content to Private Domain: Monetization on Douyin vs Xiaohongshu

I've always believed that the monetization potential of emotional short videos is wildly underestimated.

A lot of creators in the emotional content space complain that these videos get plenty of likes but don't actually make money — and that's true, but only half the story. Emotional content ranks in the top three categories for user retention.

## Xiaohongshu: Search-Driven, High-Value Conversion

Silent Diary's core metric: 63% of traffic comes from search. That means people aren't just stumbling onto her content — they're looking for it.

What these seekers have in common: high purchase intent, strong spending power (search traffic's average order value is 2.3× that of recommendation traffic), and high retention (unfollow rate is only 40% of recommendation traffic).

Conversion path: Content → Search mindshare → Comments section → DMs/shop → Private domain

## Douyin: Algorithm-Driven, Spiral Breakouts

Douyin's conversion logic is completely different. Users on Douyin don't have a search habit (at least not for emotional content), so your content has to rely on the algorithm's recommendations.

Conversion path: Viral content → Algorithm push → Live stream storefront → Fan group → Private domain

The key here is the live stream — without a live stream room, your Douyin content is basically wasted traffic.

## Data Comparison

| Metric | Xiaohongshu | Douyin |
|--------|------------|--------|
| 1,000 views → private domain | 8-15 people | 2-5 people |
| Average order value | ¥200-500 | ¥50-150 |
| Fan lifecycle | 6-12 months | 1-3 months |
| Value per follower | ¥20-50 | ¥3-8 |

## A Word of Caution

Emotional content followers hate being sold courses. The solution: don't sell anything within your emotional content. Emotional content is purely for driving traffic — save the selling for elsewhere.
