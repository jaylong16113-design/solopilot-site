# Where AI Fails at Emotional Content: Hard Lessons from a Real Creator

I use at least four AI tools every day for short video work. But when it comes to emotional content, I've taken some painful hits.

The worst one: I used AI to write a complete storyboard, generated the voiceover with AI, created background images with AI art — zero human involvement throughout. The top comment? "This feels so fake."

## What AI Is Actually Good At: The Emotion-Free Parts

**1. Topic discovery and keyword research**
Every day, I run Xiaohongshu and Douyin trending topics through AI to find today's hottest emotional keywords. Tools: opencli + ChatGPT

**2. Copy drafts and headline optimization**
I have AI analyze the sentence structures of Silent Diary's last 100 headlines, then generate 20 similar-but-different titles. Tool: Claude

**3. Editing assistance**
AI analyzes high-retention videos, marks the transition points between emotional buildup and emotional release, and generates reusable editing rhythm templates.

## What AI Is Terrible At: Anything Requiring Authenticity

**1. Camera language design**
AI can tell you to use close-ups for emotional impact, but it has no clue what kind of lighting or angle creates genuine emotional punch.

**2. Voiceover and narration**
The soul of emotional content lives in the subtle pauses and breaths. Only a real person can deliver that right now.

**3. Shot selection**
AI always picks the sharpest-looking frames — not the most natural-looking ones. This part is surprisingly hard to automate.

## My Current Workflow

- **Topic** → AI generates 5 keywords → I pick 1
- **Copy** → AI generates 10 headlines → I pick 3, remix, and polish
- **Storyboard** → Handwritten by me (no AI)
- **Filming** → 100% manual
- **Editing** → AI creates a rhythm template → I fine-tune
- **Voiceover** → 100% manual
- **Publishing** → AI generates title optimization options → I pick the best
- **Data** → AI analyzes and recommends improvements

**The golden rule:** AI handles data analysis and repetitive tasks. Humans make creative decisions and control the emotional pulse.
