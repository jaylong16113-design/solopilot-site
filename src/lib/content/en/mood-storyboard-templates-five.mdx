# 5 Ready-to-Use Storyboard Templates for Emotional Short Videos

Writing scripts from scratch every time is painfully inefficient. After producing dozens of emotional videos, I've boiled it down to 5 core storyboard templates that work for virtually any emotional theme. Swap in your own copy and visuals, and you're good to go.

Below, each template comes with a full shot-by-shot breakdown, timeline, narration script, and BGM recommendations. Follow them as-is for your first video.

## Template 1: Grief → Healing (Best for heartbreak, low points, and growth stories)

This is the template I use most often. The core logic: first let the audience "see themselves in the grief," then give them "a little warmth."

**Best for:** Breakups, workplace setbacks, exam failures, friendship fracture, self-doubt

**Full script (~30 sec, 200 words):**

| Time | Visual | Narration / Subtitle | BGM |
|:----|:-------|:--------------------|:----|
| 0-3s | Dim room, someone lying in bed staring at the ceiling, curtains drawn, faint light outside | "Back then, I felt like the whole world had abandoned me." | Solo piano, slow C key |
| 3-8s | Close-up of a phone screen, opening a chat with someone, typing then deleting | "I wanted to talk to someone, but didn't know what to say." | Piano continues, slightly louder |
| 8-12s | Rain outside the window, droplets sliding down the glass (close-up) | "I felt like everything I did was meaningless." | Piano + soft strings enter |
| 12-18s | Quick montage: eating takeout alone → subway alone → late-night overtime → flipping through old photos (1.5s each) | "Day after day, I just drifted through." | Strings crescendo, rhythm picks up |
| 18-22s | Sunlight streams into the room (fade from dark to bright), hands pull open the curtains | "Until one morning..." | Piano brightens into a new key, strings recede |
| 22-26s | Sunlight falls on a cup of coffee on the table, steam rising slowly | "I realized the sun had found its way in after all." | Solo piano, warm rhythm |
| 26-30s | A person sitting quietly by the window drinking coffee, calm expression, slight smile | "Turns out the sun didn't come out — I finally opened the curtains." | Piano fades to finish |

**Recommended BGM:** Ludovico Einaudi — *Nuvole Bianche* (first half), Ryuichi Sakamoto — *Merry Christmas Mr. Lawrence* (slow version)
**Pro tips:** 6-12s is the emotional low point — use cool tones (blue, gray). 18-22s is the emotional turn — transition from cold to warm within 1-2 seconds using CapCut's "color tone" keyframes.

## Template 2: Surprise → Gratitude (Perfect for confessions, reunions, and heartfelt gifts)

This template moves faster than the grief series because "surprise" itself demands pace.

**Best for:** Surprise proposals, unexpected reunions, heartwarming gifts, being remembered

**Full script (~25 sec, 180 words):**

| Time | Visual | Narration / Subtitle | BGM |
|:----|:-------|:--------------------|:----|
| 0-2s | Walking briskly down the street (first-person POV) | "That day was supposed to be just another ordinary day." | Upbeat guitar intro |
| 2-5s | Someone sitting on a bench looking at their phone (from behind) → looks up | "Nothing special, just like always." | Guitar + light percussion |
| 5-10s | Phone buzzes, a message appears (screen close-up), expression shifts from calm to surprised | "Then I got that message." | Music cuts out for half a second |
| 10-18s | A hug (slow motion), sunlight filtering through leaves, scattered warm lights | "In that moment, I realized the world isn't so bad after all." | Strings surge in, emotional peak |
| 18-25s | Two people walking side by side on a dusky street, long shadows trailing behind | "There's always someone, somewhere you can't see, quietly loving you." | Strings soften, gentle close |

**Recommended BGM:** *Interstellar* theme (closing section), Coldplay — *Fix You* (instrumental)
**Pro tips:** The half-second music pause at the 5-second mark is crucial — it's the pivot from "ordinary" to "surprise." Let silence breathe for half a second, then let the music surge in as the visual switches to the climax. Emotional impact doubles.

## Template 3: Longing → Reunion (Ideal for long-distance love, graduation, nostalgia, family)

The heart of longing videos is "distance" — physical distance and emotional distance. Use plenty of empty shots and slow motion to build that sense of separation.

**Best for:** Long-distance relationships, graduation separation, family far away, remembering someone

**Full script (~35 sec, 250 words):**

| Time | Visual | Narration / Subtitle | BGM |
|:----|:-------|:--------------------|:----|
| 0-4s | Empty platform, a train slowly departing (wide shot, slow motion) | "After you left, the city felt so empty." | Slow piano, extremely soft |
| 4-8s | Opening the fridge, seeing a half-finished drink | "Your drink is still in the fridge." | Piano continues |
| 8-12s | Late night, phone on the pillow, screen lit (waiting for a message) | "I always wake up in the middle of the night and check my phone out of habit." | Piano + faint ambient sound |
| 12-18s | Flashback montage: eating together, walking together, laughing together (fast cuts, 1.5s each, warm filter) | "Sometimes I think about those days." | Music briefly shifts into a memory melody |
| 18-24s | Dining table for one, empty seat across | "Eating alone, I always end up ordering enough for two without realizing it." | Music returns to the longing theme |
| 24-30s | A new station, a train pulling in, someone steps off, they lock eyes | "But I know that all this waiting is for —" | Music builds, shifts toward hope |
| 30-35s | Reunion embrace (slow motion), sunset light on both of them | "— a better reunion." | Warm musical passage, soft close |

**Recommended BGM:** Max Richter — *On the Nature of Daylight*, Joe Hisaishi — *One Summer's Day*
**Pro tips:** This template lives and dies by its transitions. When switching from reality to memory, use a "blur + warm color" filter transition. Reverse it when coming back to reality. In CapCut, set two keyframes with different "filter intensity" and "color tone" values — done.

## Template 4: Monologue → Connection (Perfect for self-growth, inner monologue, diary-style content)

This is the go-to template for the "first-person monologue" persona. The entire video feels like someone is "talking" to the camera — not literally speaking, but through visuals and narration.

**Best for:** Anxiety, social anxiety, growing pains, self-identity

**Full script (~20 sec, 150 words):**

| Time | Visual | Narration / Subtitle | BGM |
|:----|:-------|:--------------------|:----|
| 0-3s | Black screen, one line of white text slowly appears | "Have you ever felt like you're pretending to be someone you're not?" | White noise (rain or wind) |
| 3-8s | Someone from behind — walking through a crowd, everyone hurrying past, only they've stopped | "Every day I play the role of a 'normal person.' But when I'm alone, I finally feel like I can breathe." | White noise + faint piano |
| 8-13s | Late-night room, a small desk lamp on, someone curled up on the couch writing | "The things I can't say out loud, I write in my journal." | Piano melody enters |
| 13-16s | The writing hand pauses, gaze drifts to the window | "Later I realized so many people feel the same way." | Piano gently ascends |
| 16-20s | Morning light, someone drinking coffee by the window (first-person POV), a faint smile | "It's okay. You don't have to be perfect. Just be you." | Piano closes |

**Recommended BGM:** Pure rain sounds + minimalist piano, Brian Eno — *An Ending (Ascent)*
**Pro tips:** This template works with minimal visuals — you can even rotate just 3-4 shots. The key is leaving "breathing room" between each line of narration. Don't rush to the next sentence — pause 1-2 seconds and let the emotion land.

## Template 5: Contrast → Twist (Best for "seems like A but is actually B" narratives)

This is the hardest template, but it delivers the biggest impact. The core logic: build one direction, then flip it with a completely unexpected twist. The audience's emotional journey goes: "Huh? → Oh! → That's brilliant."

**Best for:** Cold-on-the-outside-warm-on-the-inside people, apparent failures who succeeded, kindness between strangers

**Full script (~35 sec, 220 words):**

| Time | Visual | Narration / Subtitle | BGM |
|:----|:-------|:--------------------|:----|
| 0-5s | Empty office late at night, only one desk lamp on | "They said he was too introverted for this job." | Slow, tense strings |
| 5-10s | Someone working at their computer, no one around | "Too nervous to speak up in meetings, always hiding in the corner at team building." | Strings sustained, mood low |
| 10-15s | Desk piled with notebooks, sticky notes, printed documents | "Everyone thought he wouldn't make it." | Music stays in the low register |
| 15-22s | Fast montage: coding late at night → studying on weekends → practicing speeches at dawn → heaps of notes and drafts (1.5-2s each, pace accelerating) | "What they didn't know was..." | Music starts accelerating, percussion enters |
| 22-28s | Photos of him speaking on stage, a trophy, smiling while discussing with his team (footage shifts from black-and-white to color) | "Sometimes, quiet people —" | Drums explode, synthesizer enters at climax |
| 28-35s | Him standing by the window, sunlight on his face, smiling | "— just spend more time shining where no one's watching." | Music falls from climax, warm close |

**Recommended BGM:** Hans Zimmer — *Time* (slow build to explosive climax), Daft Punk — *Contact* (second half)
**Pro tips:** The secret of the contrast template is "the setup needs to be long enough." The longer the "repressed" section, the more impactful the twist. I recommend spending the first 60% of the runtime on tension, and the last 40% on the turn and payoff. If you rush the setup, the twist won't land.

## How to Reuse One Script Across Multiple Videos

You don't need to rewrite scripts from scratch every time. Save these 5 templates in your CapCut "My Templates" folder. Each time you make a new video, do just three things:

1. Identify which emotional type your topic fits
2. Apply the matching template and revise the narration
3. Swap in new visuals and BGM

For the "Grief → Healing" template, you can adapt it to:
- Breakup version: living alone after a split
- Work version: finding direction after being laid off
- Study version: rebuilding confidence after failing an exam
- Friendship version: from misunderstanding to reconciliation

The core structure stays the same — "low point → shift → recovery" — only the visuals and copy change. The point of templates isn't to copy-paste. It's to free you from structuring every piece from zero, so you can focus on polishing the copy and imagery. I used to spend 40 minutes writing each script. Now, with this template library, it takes 10 minutes per script.
