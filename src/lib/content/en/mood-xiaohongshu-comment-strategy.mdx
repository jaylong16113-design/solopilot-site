# Xiaohongshu Comment Strategy for Mood Videos: 3 Steps to Get Users Saving Your Posts

Here's a striking stat: Silent Diary's average save rate is ~25% of likes — nearly double the category average of 10-15%.

The gap comes 90% from comment strategy. Not the content itself — it's the hooks she plants in the comment section.

## Step 1: Pin the Right Comment — The Emotional Trigger

Mood video comment sections have a natural confessional quality. Viewers haven't fully spent the emotional charge from the video — they come to the comments looking for shared feelings.

**The most effective pinned comment formula:** [One-line mood description] + [A small question] + [A reason to save]

**Example:** *That's just how life is — some moments just feel so quiet. Do you ever have moments like these? Save this post and pull it up next time you need it.*

This one comment contains: empathy, interaction trigger, and a saving motivation. All three, one shot.

## Step 2: Build Emotional Resonance Chains in Your Replies

Mood video comment sections have a unique phenomenon: top-voted comments aren't complaints or compliments — they're people sharing their own stories.

**How to execute:**
1. The first 30 minutes after posting are golden. Refresh comments every 5 minutes.
2. When you see a personal-story comment, reply with a short empathetic sentence.
3. When you see short reactions like "crying," "that's so me," "too real" — just hit like.

## Step 3: Save Guidance — The Detail 80% of Creators Miss

Next to the save button on Xiaohongshu (小红书, China's lifestyle platform), there's a secondary action: "Save to Collection." If you can guide users to save to a specific collection, save rates jump 30-50%.

**The core logic:** Users aren't unwilling to save — the friction is deciding *where* to save it. Make that decision for them, and they'll hit the button.

## Summary

The underlying logic of comment section strategy is simple: mood video users aren't here to watch and leave. They're here to find their people. Build them a stage, and they'll do the rest themselves.
